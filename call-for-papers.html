Call for Papers

Submission deadline: 29 August 2026 (AoE) Submission portal: OpenReview

We invite submissions to RoboPAD: Post-Training Adaptation of Robot Foundation Models, to be held as part of NeurIPS 2026 in Paris, France.

RoboPAD focuses on post-training as an algorithmic stage after pretraining: the closed-loop process of adapting, correcting, evaluating, and improving robot foundation models under real-world constraints. The central question is: after a robot foundation model has been pretrained, how should it be adapted, corrected, personalized, evaluated, and safely improved?

We welcome contributions that connect algorithmic methods for adaptation with evaluation principles for robust, interactive, and safety-critical embodied systems—including work in progress, under-review work, and recent unpublished or preprint work substantially reframed as open-problem, benchmark, or perspective contributions.

Submission Tracks

We invite contributions in the Topics of Interest that are central to the workshop theme. The topic list is not exhaustive; we also welcome submissions in related areas. Submissions may be made to either of the following tracks:

  • Short papers (up to 4 pages) — preliminary findings, position papers, benchmark analyses, infrastructure reports, and negative results that would benefit from community feedback.
  • Long papers (up to 9 pages) — mature, full-length contributions with more extensive technical development, experiments, or analysis.

Page limits exclude references and appendices. Any appendix must be included in the same PDF; separate supplementary files will not be accepted. Reviewers are not required to read the appendix. Please use the official NeurIPS 2026 LaTeX style files in workshop format.

Submission Guidelines

We will manage paper submissions through OpenReview. Reviews are double-blind, with each paper receiving three reviews; final acceptance decisions are made by the organizing committee with clear conflict-of-interest enforcement. All accepted papers will be non-archival, consistent with NeurIPS workshop policy, and will be presented in poster sessions. We will additionally select a subset of papers for contributed talks / spotlights and one Best Paper Award.

OpenReview profile: All authors and reviewers must have an OpenReview profile by the submission deadline. New profiles created without an institutional email can take up to two weeks to be moderated and activated; profiles created with an institutional email are activated automatically.

Novelty and dual submission: We welcome work in progress, under-review work, and recent unpublished or preprint work substantially reframed as open-problem, benchmark, or perspective contributions. Work already published in archival conference or journal proceedings is not eligible for submission. Authors are responsible for ensuring that workshop submission is compatible with the policies of any other venue involved. Work accepted to the NeurIPS 2026 main conference will not be presented at the workshop.

Anonymity: Submissions should be anonymized for double-blind review.

LLM policy (authors): Authors may use LLM-based tools in accordance with the NeurIPS 2026 author policy. Authors remain fully responsible for the correctness and originality of all submitted content. Any substantive use of LLMs as part of the research methodology must be clearly disclosed. Prompt injection or attempts to manipulate the review process are strictly prohibited.

LLM policy (reviewers): Reviewers are not permitted to upload submissions or submission content to external LLM services.

Appendix: Page limits exclude references and appendices. Any appendix must be included in the same PDF; separate supplementary files will not be accepted. Reviewers are not required to read the appendix.

Publication: Accepted papers will be made publicly available on OpenReview unless the authors opt out at the time of submission. They will remain non-archival and will not appear in the official NeurIPS proceedings.

In-person presentation: At least one author of each accepted paper is expected to attend the workshop in person and present the work. Acceptance does not include NeurIPS registration or travel support.

Topics of Interest

The six themes below organize the main algorithmic problems in robot-foundation-model post-training. We welcome submissions on any related topic that engages substantively with adaptation, evaluation, and improvement after pretraining.

1. Feedback- and intervention-driven adaptation

  • Learning from human corrections, preferences, teleoperation, and shared autonomy
  • Online interventions, failure recovery, and interactive policy improvement
  • Preference learning and human-in-the-loop specialization after pretraining

2. Policy optimization and specialization after pretraining

  • RL fine-tuning of VLAs and pretrained robot policies
  • Offline-to-online improvement, action decoders, and in-context policy adaptation
  • Fast specialization with limited real-world data

3. World-model- and simulation-driven adaptation

  • Adapting latent dynamics, predictive models, and task-level models
  • Simulation-generated data for post-training under changing environments
  • Closed-loop refinement of world models and policies before real-world deployment

4. Reasoning, memory, and agentic robot adaptation

  • Coupling deliberative multimodal reasoning with reactive control
  • Long-term robot memory, task decomposition, and when-to-think decisions
  • Agentic pipelines for correction, replanning, and recovery

5. Cross-embodiment and whole-body adaptation

  • Humanoids, dexterous hands, mobile manipulators, and embodiment transfer
  • Specialist distillation, sim-to-real adaptation, and safety-aware post-training
  • Whole-body and multi-robot specialization after pretraining

6. Evaluation, safety, and robustness after adaptation

  • Reproducible protocols, robustness under distribution shift, and recovery metrics
  • Constraint-respecting fine-tuning, reliability, and long-horizon evaluation
  • Benchmarks and reporting practices for post-training outcomes
